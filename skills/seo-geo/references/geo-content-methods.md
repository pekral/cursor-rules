# GEO Content Methods (Princeton-style Checklist)

## Core Priorities

Prioritize these content signals for AI citation visibility:

1. **Authoritative citations** — reference credible sources inline
2. **Concrete statistics** — use specific numbers, data points, percentages
3. **Attributed quotations** — quote experts with name and role
4. **Confident expert tone** — write as a subject-matter authority
5. **Plain-language explanations** — make complex topics accessible
6. **Domain terminology** — use precise terms where appropriate
7. **Varied vocabulary** — avoid repetition; use synonyms and related terms
8. **Strong readability (fluency)** — short sentences, clear structure

## Strong Pair

- **Fluency + statistics** — combining readable prose with hard numbers yields the best citation performance.

## Anti-patterns

- **Keyword stuffing** — hurts visibility across both traditional and AI search
- **Vague claims without evidence** — AI systems prefer verifiable statements
- **Wall-of-text paragraphs** — poor readability reduces citation likelihood

## Content Layout

- **Answer-first** — provide the direct answer before elaborating with detail
- **Clear heading hierarchy** — `H1` > `H2` > `H3`, no skipped levels
- **Lists and tables** — use for comparisons, feature breakdowns, and structured data
- **Short paragraphs** — 2-4 sentences per paragraph for scannability
